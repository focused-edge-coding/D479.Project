Taniti is a small, tropical island in the Pacific.

The island has an area of less than 500 square miles. The terrain is varied
and includes both sandy and rocky beaches, a small, safe harbor, lush tropical
rainforests, and a mountainous interior that includes a small active volcano.
Taniti has an indigenous population of about 20,000 people. Until a recent increase
in tourism, most of the Tanitian economy was dominated by fishing or agriculture.

Taniti has 2 supermarkets, 2 regular grocery stores, and 1 convenience store that is open 24 hours a day.
